Biology Major

Clinical Volunteer Experience: Since August 2023, I have been actively involved as a volunteer in Cedar Sinai's Transforming Care at Bedside (TCAB) Program in Los Angeles, CA. This role has afforded me the opportunity to engage in direct patient care under the guidance of experienced nurses and healthcare professionals, enhancing my patient interaction skills and bedside manner. I am responsible for managing medical equipment inventory to ensure seamless healthcare operations across various departments. My role extends to coordinating blood work logistics for efficient lab deliveries while adhering to strict safety and procedural standards. Additionally, I facilitate communication within the healthcare framework, directing calls and conveying critical information to the staff, and I partake in interdisciplinary team meetings, expanding my comprehension of diverse healthcare roles and the intricacies of patient care dynamics.

Educational Pursuit in Quality Science: In the Spring of 2022, I completed the 'Pathway for Patient Health: Quality Science Education Course' in Los Angeles, CA, where I developed an in-depth understanding of life science regulations, including the U.S. Code of Federal Regulations (CFR), EudraLex Directives, and Emerging Market regulations. This course allowed me to explore the nexus between regulatory mandates, legal accountability, and clinical trial oversight, emphasizing data integrity and security. Through case studies, I gained practical insight into the applications of ethical regulations and change control in the life sciences sector. This educational experience has equipped me with the knowledge to enhance patient health outcomes and advance business objectives through regulatory acumen, quality effectiveness, and scientific rigor.

Emergency Medical Training: During the Summer of 2022, I honed my emergency medical skills as an EMT Trainee with West Coast EMT in Anaheim, CA. I gained practical experience in patient assessments and basic life support techniques, such as CPR and first aid, in a fast-paced ambulance setting. My training involved mastering the use of essential medical equipment and adopting critical patient management skills, including triage and emergency transport. A unique contribution I made was utilizing my Spanish language skills to communicate effectively with patients, a service invaluable to non-Spanish speaking crew members. The rigorous program included simulated emergency scenarios, preparing me for real-world interventions, and an intensive study of physiology and anatomy, underpinning my hands-on experiences.
